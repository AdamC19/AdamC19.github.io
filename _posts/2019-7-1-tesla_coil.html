Overview

I got interested in builing a Tesla coil when I saw YouTuber ElectroBOOM's videos on Tesla coils. I was also influenced by the OneTesla design (which ElectroBOOM reviews on his channel). What I wanted was a circuit that would play high fidelity music, similar to ElectroBOOM's design and unlike the OneTesla design. It needed to be solid state, and naturally I wanted the biggest arcs possible.

Like ElectroBOOM's design, I planned for my driver to be a continuous wave type. My primary requirement for my control circuit was that it shall lock on to the resonant frequency of the secondary coil. ElectroBOOM notes that the lack of this feature is a shortcoming of his design. The OneTesla design also lacks secondary feedback, instead opting for a primary LC circuit that resonates close enough to the nominal resonant frequency of the tower.
